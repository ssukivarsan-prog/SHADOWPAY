The Person We're Building For

Not vaguely. For Rajan specifically.

Last October during the northeast monsoon, one of our team member's uncle lost nearly ₹6,000 in a single week because he simply couldn't ride.

His name is Rajan. 27 years old, rides a Honda Activa in Anna Nagar, Chennai. Works Zomato. On a good day he makes ₹700–800.

When it rains heavily, he doesn't just slow down — he stops completely. The roads flood. He can't see. The app keeps pinging with orders he can't take. His weekly earnings dropped from ₹4,200 to ₹2,640. With rent at ₹3,200 and bike EMI at ₹1,100, he had to ask his mother for money.

There's no insurance that covers this. Zomato Shield is accident-only. Vehicle insurance doesn't pay because the bike is fine — the problem is the sky.

The Shadow Earnings Model

We trigger on outcomes (lost income), not just events.

Every 15 minutes, our XGBoost model predicts Rajan's "Shadow Earnings" — what he should be earning right now based on:

  • Hour of day & day of week (lunch vs dinner)
  • Personal historical average for this slot
  • Active riders in zone (supply vs demand)
  • Weather status & local events

When actual earnings fall 35%+ below the shadow prediction AND a disruption is active, we pay 80% of the gap automatically. Under 15 minutes.

payout_engine.py 
shadow = shadow_model.predict(worker_id, current_time)
actual = platform_api.get_earnings(worker_id, last_2h)
gap    = (shadow - actual) / shadow

if gap > 0.35 and disruption_active and trust_score < 70:
    payout = min((shadow - actual) * 0.80, weekly_cap)
    send_to_worker(worker_id, payout)

Adversarial Defense

Solving the "GPS Syndicate" problem. We stop trusting location and trust convergent physical evidence.

1. Sensor Fusion Pipeline

We read barometric pressure, humidity, and the microphone for rain signatures. A Random Forest checks if the phone environment actually matches a rainstorm.

2. Peer Consensus

Are 3+ workers within 500m also losing income right now? If only one person's earnings drop during a "flood", it's suspicious.

3. Behavioral Motion (LSTM)

A stranded partner checking their phone in the rain has a different accelerometer signature than someone sitting still at home spoofing GPS.

4. Platform Ground Truth

If the delivery platform itself isn't showing a massive drop in order assignment rates in that zone, the disruption isn't real.
